Join us for an Easter discussion: How have different traditions understood the story of the resurrection of Jesus of Nazareth?

Speaker: Nicholas Piotrowski

Christians have been celebrating Good Friday and Easter for nearly 2,000 years. But why? That Jesus of Nazareth was executed is a given, but what does the Easter holiday celebrate? Was Jesus really raised from the dead? Was that even the message first Christians sought to spread? Or did they know, even then, that this was a metaphorical tale? How has the story been understood since those first days of the Christian movement? Join us as we consider how different traditions have historically understood the story of the resurrection of Jesus of Nazareth. All are welcome!

Our Speaker

Nicholas Piotrowski Nicholas is a resident of Lawrence and a professor at Crossroads Bible College. He earned his PhD at Wheaton College where he studied the way 1st century communities used sacred traditions to form their self-identity and navigate their cultural milieu. This upcoming talk and discussion will revolve around only a slightly different topic. At the end of the 19th century Friedrich Nietzsche also used religious texts to give shape to the burgeoning postmodern world when he proclaimed that “God is dead.” Western thinkers in every discipline from political theory to the fine arts have taken up this “death of God” motif throughout the 20th century. Nicholas has observed, however, that what Nietzsche specifically meant has been lost in the intervening years. It seems time, therefore, to reevaluate Nietzsche’s famous pronouncement in the light of more than a century’s worth of retrospect. Join us on January 18th at 10:30am at the Lawrence Public Library for a lecture and open discussion/Q&A as we consider what it means that “God is dead.”
